Concepts  
Conceptual errors will mostly be related to the concepts being taught and  
not the subject in general. Focus on the concepts being taught in the  
chapter. (However, any concept from outside the chapters will also be  
considered here.)  
All errors that a teacher makes, whether verbally or in any other way, to be  
taxed in this parameter.  
If a student utters a conceptual / subject language error, but the teacher  
restates this, then it is to be considered the teacher's error.  
For each error, mention the correct concept in the [square brackets].  
For English, word meanings are also to be taxed here

This looks at the correct word, phrase, terms that a subject requires.  
We primarily look at the 'technical' language that is required in a subject.  
Spellings, capitalization, etc. of subject-specific terms are also to be  
checked here  
For English, word/phrase usage is considered subject language

If a teacher is unprepared with a resource and has to run around to make it  
available, this will NOT be taxed under Time Utilisation. However, if any  
time wastage goes beyond 5 minutes, the teacher will be taxed in the Time  
Utilisation parameter.  
Something like an AC remote is technically not a classroom resource. If the  
teacher sends a student out to get the remote and ensures that this student  
does not miss any part of the session, then the teacher cannot be taxed. But  
when the student is out, and the teacher continues teaching, then the  
teacher should be taxed.
